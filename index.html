How Image Compression Works — By Format

📸 JPEG Compression

JPEG uses lossy compression — it removes details the human eye can't easily see. Our tool detects the original quality level and lets you choose the output size. Best for: photos, web images, social media. Not ideal for: text-heavy images, logos with sharp edges.

Compress JPEG →

🎨 PNG Optimization

PNG uses lossless compression — no quality is lost. Our tool reduces PNG size by converting to an optimized palette while preserving transparency. Best for: logos, icons, screenshots, graphics. Not ideal for: photographs (use JPEG instead).

Compress PNG →

🌐 WebP Compression

WebP is the modern web format — files are 25-35% smaller than JPEG at the same quality. Our tool supports both lossy and lossless WebP compression. Best for: websites, Core Web Vitals optimization. Not ideal for: apps that don't support WebP.

🔤 Icons & Logos

Icons and logos need tiny file sizes — under 50KB for fast loading. Our tool optimizes palette colors and removes metadata for maximum compression while keeping sharp edges. Best for: favicons, app icons, brand logos.
